CONSENT FORM - User Experience of Interactive Architecture


You are being asked to take part in a research study to investigate inhabitant’s perceptions and experience of Interactive Architecture under different modes of interaction. If you agree to participate, you will be asked to experience an interactive lounge space and answer a survey about your opinions and experience.

The interactive lounge prototype is an interactive architecture system where a building’s physical walls are replaced by virtual walls. You will only experience and report on one mode of interaction with this space. You are not expected to return another time for trying different modes. While you’re welcome to return, your data as participant will only be used in the first day you participate.

This study is part of Sara C. Maia’s master’s thesis’ research, under the supervision of Prof. AnnaLisa Meyboom (Principal Investigator). Please read this form carefully and ask any questions you may have before agreeing to take part in the study.

What the study is about:

The purpose of this study is to learn about user experience of Interactive Architecture under different modes of interaction typically discussed in the literature of the field.

What we will ask you to do:

If you agree to participate, you will be asked to experience an interactive lounge space for an indeterminate period of time. You can stay there for as long as you want, as there is no minimum or maximum time established.

You will not be required to perform any specific task or activity while inside the space. Instead, you can use the lounge regularly for studying, relaxing, meeting friends, or any activity you deem appropriate.

You’ll be requested to fill a brief questionnaire before receiving an access code to enter the interactive lounge. This initial questionnaire will take you 2 minutes to complete. You’ll also be requested to fill a user experience survey after you leave the interactive lounge. The survey will take you 6 minutes to complete.

Additionally, you must be aware that an investigator in the field will observe participants’ interaction with the space and keep anonymous notes about the observations. Nothing will be recorded or annotated that will allow you be identified personally.

Time required to participate:

Complete participation in this study is expected to take a minimum of 8 minutes to complete.

Risks and benefits:

The risks involved in this study are no greater than those involved in occupying regular architectural spaces. There are no monetary benefits or compensations to you for participating, however we will be serving snacks (subject to availability, while stocks last) and coffee at no cost for participants.

Taking part is voluntary:

Taking part in this study is completely voluntary. If you decide to take part, you are free to withdraw at any time. In the surveys, you may also skip any questions that you do not want to answer, as well as the entire survey, without any sort of penalty.

If you have questions:

The Principal Investigator conducting this study is AnnaLisa Meyboom. If you have questions now or later, you may contact the researchers below in the following email addresses.

AnnaLisa Meyboom: ameyboom@sala.ubc.ca

Sara C. Maia: sara.maia@alumni.ubc.ca

If you have complaints or concerns about the study:

If you have any concerns or complaints about your rights as a research participant and/or your experiences while participating in this study, contact the Research Participant Complaint Line in the UBC Office of Research Ethics at 604-822-8598 or if long distance e-mail RSIL@ors.ubc.ca or call toll free 1-877-822-8598.
